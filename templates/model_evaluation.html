Section 06 — Risk Analysis

Risks & Potential Biases

Even with high accuracy, the model carries inherent risks that must be understood before relying on its predictions in operational contexts.

01
Synthetic Data Bias
The model trains entirely on synthetic data. While correlations are realistic, it may not generalize perfectly to real-world edge cases not captured during generation.
02
Class Imbalance
The LOW class has fewer records (830) vs HIGH (2,633) and MEDIUM (3,037), which may reduce recall on rare low-risk cases in unbalanced real data.
03
Train/Test Overlap
Accuracy of {{ accuracy }}% is measured on training data. Cross-validation ({{ cv_mean }}%) provides a more conservative and trustworthy real-world estimate.
04
Geographic Scope
Dataset covers only Bogotá localities. Applying the model to other cities may yield unreliable predictions without retraining on local data.
05
Temporal Drift
A model trained on 2021–2024 data may degrade in accuracy as conditions evolve. Periodic retraining with updated data is required to maintain performance.
06
Microchip Dominance
Microchip coverage accounts for 44% of feature importance. If this variable is unavailable or inaccurate, prediction quality drops significantly.
